Online Adaptive Anomaly Detection for Augmented Network Flows

Online Adaptive Anomaly Detection for Augmented Network Flows

论文链接：https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/7033682

Abstract

传统的网络异常检测涉及开发依赖于数据包检测的模型。提高网络速度和使用加密协议使得每个数据包的检测不适合当今的网络。克服这一障碍的一种方法是基于流量的分析。许多现有方法具有特殊用途，即仅限于检测特定行为。此外，识别异常流所固有的数据缩减会阻碍警报关联。在本文中，我们提出了一种用于增强流的动态异常检测方法。我们在流创建期间绘制网络状态的草图，以实现通用威胁检测。我们设计和开发了一种基于支持向量机的自适应异常检测和关联机制，该机制能够在没有先验警报分类和在线进化模型的情况下聚合警报。我们开发了一种置信转发机制，用于识别用于额外处理的一小部分预测。我们在企业和骨干跟踪上都展示了我们方法的有效性。实验结果表明，无需离线训练即可保持高精度。

传统的网络异常检测包括开发依赖于数据包检查的模型。然而，不断提高的网络速度和加密协议的使用使得每个数据包的检查不适合今天的网络。克服这一障碍的一种方法是聚合包头信息并执行基于流的分析，其中检查数据流模式，而不是深度包检查。许多现有的方法都是局限于检测特定行为的特殊目的。此外，识别异常流所固有的数据减少也阻碍了警报相关性。本文提出并开发了一种增强网络流的动态异常检测方法。我们绘制了流创建过程中的网络状态，实现了通用的威胁检测。我们描述了一种基于计数最小草图的高效流增强方法，该方法提供了与流记录生成并行的每个流、每个节点和每个网络级的统计信息。我们设计并开发了一种基于支持向量机的自适应异常检测和相关机制，它能够在不进行先验警报分类的情况下聚合警报。

Traditional network anomaly detection involves developing models that rely on packet inspection. However,

increasing network speeds and use of encrypted protocols make per-packet inspection unsuited for today’s

networks. One method of overcoming this obstacle is aggregating packet header information and perform

ing flflow-based analysis where data flflow patterns are examined rather than deep packet inspection. Many

existing approaches are special purpose limited to detecting specifific behavior. Also, the data reduction in

herent in identifying anomalous flflows hinders alert correlation. In this article, we propose and develop a

dynamic anomaly detection approach for augmented network flflows. We sketch network state during flflow

creation, enabling general-purpose threat detection. We describe an effificient flflow augmentation approach

based on the count-min sketch that provides per-flflow-, per-node-, and per-network-level statistics parallel to

flflow record generation. We design and develop a support vector machine-based adaptive anomaly detection

and correlation mechanism, which is capable of aggregating alerts without a priori alert classifification and

evolving models online. We further develop a lightweight evolving alert aggregation method and combine it

with a confifidence forwarding mechanism identifying a small percentage predictions for additional process

ing. We show effectiveness of our methods on both enterprise and backbone traces. Experimental results

demonstrate its ability to maintain high accuracy without the need for offlfline training
